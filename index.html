The Problem

Mineral Processing Plants Are Flying Blind

Slurry circuits are the heart of any processing operation — but the variables that govern circuit performance — flow, density, rheology, particle size, entrained air — are routinely unmeasured, mis-measured, or measured hours after the fact.

The instruments that exist today each fall short in a different way.

Radioactive density gauges

They work — but carry nuclear licensing, safety, and decommissioning overhead that operators are under growing pressure to eliminate.

Magnetic flow meters

Fail on low-conductivity slurries — which describes most mineral processing streams.

Intrusive probes

Wear out in the same abrasive conditions they're meant to monitor — and require process shutdown to replace.

Manual sampling

Introduces hours of lag. Covering even the basics requires multiple instruments from multiple vendors — each with its own installation, calibration, and maintenance burden.

The result: process control decisions worth millions of dollars are made on incomplete, lagged, or missing data. Equipment fails before it needs to. Recovery rates are managed conservatively when they could be tighter. And new greenfield projects come online with no historical process data at all.

The measurement problem isn't a niche gap. It's an industry-wide blind spot — and no single platform addresses it today.
