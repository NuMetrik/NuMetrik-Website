Solutions · Beyond Mining

Other Industries

NuMetrik is building the process intelligence platform for natural resource industries, starting in mineral processing. The physics that works in mineral processing also works across a broader range of slurry-handling and pipeline applications.

Where We're Starting vs. Where We're Going

Mineral Processing First — Natural Resources Broadly

NuMetrik's current commercial focus is mineral processing, specifically flotation circuits, hydrocyclone feed, tailings transfer, and the measurement challenges specific to those applications. This is where we are building validated performance data and initial commercial deployments.

The underlying physics (transit-time ultrasonic measurement applied externally to pipes) has applications across a wider range of industries wherever slurry is moved through pipes and where non-intrusive, continuous measurement would provide operational value. We signal that vision here, without overstating our current position in adjacent sectors.

Current market focus: Mineral processing: gold, copper, nickel, lithium, and base metals processing operations. Validation data and commercial deployments are specific to this sector. Adjacent sectors listed below are a vision signal, not a current commercial claim.

Adjacent Sectors

Where the Physics Is Relevant

These sectors share similar measurement challenges: abrasive or complex slurry in pipelines, intrusive instrument failures, and a need for continuous non-intrusive measurement.

Oil Sands & Heavy Oil

Tailings and process streams in oil sands operations involve high-density slurry where measurement is critical for mass balance, pipeline integrity, and environmental compliance. Non-intrusive measurement is directly applicable in principle.

Vision-stage. Not a current commercial focus. Requires dedicated validation for oil sands slurry conditions.

Cement & Aggregate

Slurry pipelines in cement and aggregate operations (wash water circuits, limestone slurry) present analogous non-intrusive measurement opportunities where intrusive instruments suffer accelerated wear.

Vision-stage. Not a current commercial focus.

Pulp & Paper

Pulp suspension density and flow measurement are instrumentation challenges with close parallels to mineral slurry measurement. Transit-time ultrasonics has an established application history in clean-liquor pulp measurement.

Vision-stage. Requires NuMetrik-specific validation for pulp slurry conditions.

Water & Wastewater

Municipal and industrial wastewater treatment involves sludge streams with density and flow measurement requirements. Non-intrusive measurement removes a significant maintenance burden in these environments.

Vision-stage. Not a current commercial focus.

Dredging & Marine

Dredge pipeline density measurement is a demanding application, with high abrasion, variable solids concentration, and continuous operation requirements that make intrusive instrumentation particularly costly to maintain.

Vision-stage. Not a current commercial focus.

Chemical Processing

Multi-phase flow measurement in chemical plant pipelines, where reagent management, slurry transfer, and process accounting require continuous, reliable density and flow data, shares the core measurement challenge.

Vision-stage. Not a current commercial focus.
