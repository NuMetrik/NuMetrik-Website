Environmental Impact

How real-time measurement reduces resource waste

Water Usage & Tailings Management

Accurate, continuous slurry density measurement enables tighter process control, reducing water-to-solids ratio variance and improving tailings circuit stability. Better density control means more consistent tailings moisture content and reduced water loss to the tailings pond.

Note: Slurry density measurement is currently on the R&D roadmap. See deploy-ready parameters →

Energy Efficiency & Carbon Footprint

Slurry pumping is one of the largest energy consumers in a mineral processing plant. Real-time flow measurement enables pump operating point optimization, running pumps on their efficiency curve rather than off it. The energy efficiency gain scales directly with pumping volume and operational hours.

Eliminating Nuclear Gauge Radiological Risk

The measurement system can reduce or replace reliance on nuclear gauges for slurry density measurement, helping remove radiological source licensing, reporting obligations, and eventual radioactive source disposal from the operational footprint where nuclear instruments are retired.

Compliance Reporting

Continuous data vs. grab samples — accuracy and audit trail

Environmental compliance reporting for mineral processing operations often relies on periodic grab samples, which capture conditions at isolated points in time rather than continuously. A grab sample represents one moment; a continuous measurement system can provide a fuller view of process variation over time.

The difference matters for both accuracy and defensibility:

📋

Continuous audit trail

Every measurement is timestamped and logged. Compliance reports based on continuous data are complete and unambiguous, not reconstructed from spot measurements.

🎯

Representative measurement

Continuous flow and process measurement captures excursions that periodic grab sampling misses entirely. A non-representative sample period can significantly under- or overstate actual throughput and process conditions.

✅

Traceable Calibration for Environmental Reporting

Deploy-ready measurement parameters are designed to support traceable calibration workflows, creating a stronger basis for environmental reporting than extrapolation from intermittent grab samples.

Data Trust Layer

Sensor Fusion for ESG-Grade Reporting

NuMetrik's Certainty layer applies sensor fusion and process data reconciliation to address a specific challenge in environmental reporting: when multiple measurement points characterize the same process variable, they frequently disagree. Certainty turns those conflicting inputs into a single reconciled value with a defined confidence interval.

For environmental reporting, this means one defensible number (with documented uncertainty) derived from multiple independent measurements, rather than a single instrument's reading with no redundancy or cross-validation.
