Where Value Is Created

Five Dimensions of Value

The business case for continuous slurry measurement spans five value categories. The weight of each depends on your operation: throughput, current instrumentation costs, process variability, and maintenance profile. A site-specific conversation will produce figures you can take to a capital review group.

Avoided Lab and Sampling Costs

Continuous online measurement reduces, and in some applications eliminates, the need for periodic lab grabs. The direct cost saving depends on current sampling frequency, lab cost per sample, and the labour attached to sampling rounds.

Instrumentation Maintenance Reduction

Intrusive sensors in slurry service require frequent calibration, component replacement, and planned downtime for access. Non-intrusive clamp-on measurement has no wetted parts; the ongoing maintenance cost profile is structurally different.

Throughput and Process Optimisation

Continuous real-time flow data enables tighter circuit control. Better-controlled processes run closer to their optimum, reducing variability in recovery and reagent consumption.

Reagent and Energy Efficiency

Reagent dosing informed by real-time flow data wastes less. Energy-intensive processes (grinding, pumping) can be optimised against actual circuit conditions rather than inferred or sampled ones.

Unplanned Downtime Reduction

Liner wear tracked continuously means replacements planned rather than forced. Flow anomalies detected early, before they become pump failures or pipeline incidents, reduce the unplanned downtime events that carry the largest cost per hour in mineral processing.

Return estimates are site-specific. They depend on your current instrumentation costs, sampling regime, process variability, and mineral economics. Talk to our team → to develop figures you can take to a capital review group.
